body: >-

Today, I decided to finally take on something that has been terrifying me for a while, something I've been postponing again and again: opening some of my phones, more precisely, dismantling the parts that are glued together instead of screwed.
We all remember my misadventure from a few months ago, when I almost set my house on fire at 2 a.m., while all my flatmates were asleep, trying to remove the very well-glued battery of a Huawei using a screwdriver (never again). Instead of acting as a lever, the screwdriver politely punctured the battery, which immediately started smoking and releasing some rather worrying solvent vapours.
To learn more about this adventure, click on this article.

This time, with a hairdryer

So comes the moment to open the Samsung Galaxy Trend 2. I found a YouTube video showing a step-by-step method to open it without damaging the phone. The key to not repeating my previous mistakes is to heat the screen with a hair dryer. This softens the glue and allows the different parts to be gently separated. Once the screen is loosened, a plastic insert is placed between the two parts to keep a small gap open.
